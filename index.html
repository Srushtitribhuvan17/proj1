How to Promote Gender Equity?

  • Advocate for equal rights and opportunities for all genders.
  • Challenge and change discriminatory practices and gender norms.
  • Support policies and initiatives that address gender disparities.
  • Encourage education and awareness about gender issues.
  • Ensure equal representation in decision-making processes.
  • Promote work-life balance and flexible working arrangements.

Gender Equity Examples

Gender Equity at workplace

Equity in the workplace is the idea that all employees are provided with fair and equal opportunities based on their individual needs.

  • Equal Pay and Benefits
  • Diverse Hiring and Promotions
  • Transparency in Policies and Decisions

Gender equity in Education

Gender equity in education refers to ensuring equal opportunities, resources, and treatment for individuals of all genders in educational settings.

  • Eliminate Gender Bias in Curriculum
  • Equal Access to Education
  • Monitor and Evaluate Progress

Global Gender Wealth Equity Report

What is the Wealth Equity Index?

The Wealth Equity Index – developed in collaboration with the World Economic Forum – takes a holistic view of gender inequities across women’s lifetimes and quantifies the gender wealth gap between men and women at retirement. The index substantiates that women are at considerable disadvantage in the accumulation of wealth at the end of their career.
